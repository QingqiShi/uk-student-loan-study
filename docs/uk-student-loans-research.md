## Overview of Loan Plans

The UK has five student loan repayment plans. You cannot choose your repayment plan - it is determined by when you started your course, where you applied, and what type of course you studied.

| Plan             | Who It Applies To                                   |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **Plan 1**       | England/Wales (pre-Sept 2012), all Northern Ireland |
| **Plan 2**       | England (Sept 2012 - July 2023), Wales (Sept 2012+) |
| **Plan 4**       | All Scotland                                        |
| **Plan 5**       | England only (Aug 2023+)                            |
| **Postgraduate** | Master's/Doctoral in England and Wales              |

---

## Plan 1

### Who Has Plan 1?

You're on Plan 1 if:

- You started your course **before 1 September 2012** in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland
- You're from **Northern Ireland** (regardless of when you started or course type)

### Current Thresholds (2025/26)

| Period                      | Annual  | Monthly | Weekly |
| --------------------------- | ------- | ------- | ------ |
| 6 April 2025 - 5 April 2026 | £26,065 | £2,172  | £501   |
| 6 April 2026 - 5 April 2027 | £26,900 | £2,242  | —      |

### Repayment Rate

**9%** of income above the threshold

### Interest Rate

The interest rate is the **lower** of:

- Retail Price Index (RPI)
- Bank of England base rate + 1%

**Current rate (Sept 2025 - Aug 2026):** 3.2%

Interest is charged from the day of first payment until the loan is repaid or cancelled. Rates are typically adjusted on 1 September based on the previous March's RPI.

### Write-Off Rules

| When First Loan Was Paid     | Write-Off                         |
| ---------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Before 1 September 2006      | When you turn 65                  |
| On or after 1 September 2006 | 25 years after first due to repay |

## Plan 2

### Who Has Plan 2?

You're on Plan 2 if:

- **England:** Started undergraduate, PGCE, or Advanced Learner Loan course between 1 September 2012 and 31 July 2023
- **Wales:** Started undergraduate or PGCE course on or after 1 September 2012
- **England:** Higher Education Short Course Loan (any start date)

### Current Thresholds (2025/26)

| Period                      | Annual  | Monthly | Weekly |
| --------------------------- | ------- | ------- | ------ |
| 6 April 2025 - 5 April 2026 | £28,470 | £2,372  | £547   |
| From 6 April 2026           | £29,385 | £2,449  | —      |

### Repayment Rate

**9%** of income above the threshold

### Interest Rate (Sliding Scale)

Plan 2 uses an income-based sliding scale for interest after you finish studying.

**While studying:** 6.2% (RPI + 3%)

**After finishing/leaving course (2025/26):**

| Annual Income      | Interest Rate      |
| ------------------ | ------------------ |
| £28,470 or less    | 3.2%               |
| £28,471 to £51,245 | 3.2% plus up to 3% |
| £51,245 or more    | 6.2%               |

**From April 2026:**

- Lower interest threshold: £29,385
- Higher interest threshold: £52,885

Interest rate changes occur on 6 April after you finish or leave your course.

### Write-Off

**30 years** after the April you were first due to repay

## Plan 4 (Scotland)

### Who Has Plan 4?

You're on Plan 4 if you're from **Scotland**, regardless of whether you studied an undergraduate or postgraduate course.

### Current Thresholds (2025/26)

| Period                      | Annual  | Monthly | Weekly |
| --------------------------- | ------- | ------- | ------ |
| 6 April 2025 - 5 April 2026 | £32,745 | £2,728  | £629   |

**Note:** Plan 4 has the highest threshold of all plans.

### Repayment Rate

**9%** of income above the threshold

### Interest Rate

The interest rate is the **lower** of:

- Retail Price Index (RPI)
- Bank of England base rate + 1%

**Current rate (Sept 2025 - Aug 2026):** 3.2%

### Write-Off Rules

| When First Loan Was Paid  | Write-Off                                                        |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Before 1 August 2007      | Age 65 or 30 years after first due to repay (whichever is first) |
| On or after 1 August 2007 | 30 years after first due to repay                                |

## Plan 5

### Who Has Plan 5?

You're on Plan 5 if:

- **England only:** Started undergraduate, PGCE, or Advanced Learner Loan course on or after 1 August 2023

### Thresholds

| Period          | Annual  | Monthly | Weekly |
| --------------- | ------- | ------- | ------ |
| From April 2026 | £25,000 | £2,083  | £480   |

**Note:** Plan 5 has a **lower** threshold than Plan 2, meaning repayments start at a lower income level.

### Repayment Rate

**9%** of income above the threshold

### Interest Rate

**RPI only** (no additional percentage)

**Current rate (Sept 2025 - Aug 2026):** 3.2%

This means Plan 5 borrowers will not pay back more than they borrowed in real terms.

### Write-Off

**40 years** after the April you were first due to repay

This is the longest write-off period of any plan.

### When Repayments Start

First cohort begins repayments in **April 2026**.

## Postgraduate Loans

### Who Has Postgraduate Loans?

You have a Postgraduate Loan if you took out a loan for:

- A **Master's course** in England or Wales
- A **Doctoral course** in England or Wales

This is separate from undergraduate loan plans.

### Thresholds

| Period          | Annual  | Monthly | Weekly |
| --------------- | ------- | ------- | ------ |
| Current         | £21,000 | £1,750  | £403   |
| From April 2026 | £21,000 | —       | —      |

**Note:** The postgraduate threshold has remained at £21,000 since the loan was introduced.

### Repayment Rate

**6%** of income above the threshold (lower than undergraduate loans)

### Interest Rate

**RPI + 3%**

**Current rate (Sept 2025 - Aug 2026):** 6.2%

### Write-Off

**30 years** after the April you were first due to repay

### Loan Amounts (2025/26)

**Master's Loan:**
| Course Start | Maximum |
|--------------|---------|
| On or after 1 Aug 2025 | £12,858 |
| 1 Aug 2024 - 31 July 2025 | £12,471 |

**Doctoral Loan:**
| Course Start | Maximum |
|--------------|---------|
| On or after 1 Aug 2025 | £30,301 |
| 1 Aug 2024 - 31 July 2025 | £29,390 |

The loan amount is not based on household income. Doctoral loans are paid in 3 instalments (33%, 33%, 34%).

### Concurrent Repayments

If you have both undergraduate and postgraduate loans, you repay both simultaneously:

- 9% of income over undergraduate threshold toward undergraduate loan
- 6% of income over £21,000 toward postgraduate loan

## Interest Rate Calculations

### How Interest Is Applied

- Interest is charged from the day the first payment is made until the loan is repaid or cancelled
- Interest is added to the loan monthly
- Rates are typically reviewed on **1 September** based on the previous March's RPI
- Rates can change during the year (particularly for Plan 1 and Plan 4 if BoE base rate changes)

### Plan 1 & Plan 4

```
Interest = MIN(RPI, Bank of England Base Rate + 1%)
```

For Sept 2025 - Aug 2026:

- RPI = 3.2%
- BoE Base Rate + 1% = 5.0% (approx)
- **Applied rate = 3.2%**

### Plan 2 (Sliding Scale)

**While studying:** RPI + 3% = 6.2%

**After leaving course:**

```
If income ≤ lower threshold: Interest = RPI
If income ≥ upper threshold: Interest = RPI + 3%
If income between: Linear interpolation
```

**2025/26 thresholds:**

- Lower: £28,470
- Upper: £51,245

**From April 2026:**

- Lower: £29,385
- Upper: £52,885

### Plan 5

```
Interest = RPI = 3.2%
```

No additional interest above inflation.

### Postgraduate

```
Interest = RPI + 3% = 6.2%
```

Fixed rate regardless of income.

## Repayment Mechanics

### When Repayments Start

| Situation | When Repayments Begin                                                           |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Full-time | April after you finish or leave your course                                     |
| Part-time | April 4 years after course start, or April after you leave (whichever is first) |
| Plan 5    | April 2026 (first cohort)                                                       |

Repayments only occur when income exceeds the threshold for your plan.

### How Repayments Are Collected

**Employed:** Deducted from salary through PAYE alongside tax and National Insurance

**Self-employed:** Calculated and paid through Self Assessment tax return

### Loan Cancellation

Loans are automatically cancelled in these circumstances:

- **Death:** SLC cancels upon receiving death certificate
- **Permanent disability:** May be cancelled if claiming certain disability benefits (evidence required)
- **Write-off period reached:** Automatic cancellation regardless of outstanding balance
